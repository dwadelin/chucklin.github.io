    • 2014 Microsoft-IEEE Young Fellowship Ceremony

    I was honored to be one of the three undergraduate recipients from Taiwan who received the prestigious Microsoft-IEEE Young Fellowship (formerly called Microsoft Young Fellowship) for exceptional academic and research performance in 2014. The recipients were invited to Microsoft Research Asia in Beijing for presenting their ideas in a poster section, and collaborating with other recipients from Asia to discuss research solutions.

    2013 Irving T. Ho Memorial Scholarship Award Ceremony

    I was honored to receive the Irving T. Ho Memorial Scholarship for exceptional academic and research performance in my junior and senior years (2013 and 2014). The founder of the scholarship, Dr. Laung-Terng Wang, kindly invited all recipients for a luncheon gathering in Taipei. I received the Award with another three undergraduate students in 2013: Mr. Tzu-Chieh Chou (now at Caltech), Ms. Guan-Lin Chao (now at CMU), and Mr. Yung-Hsu Yang (now at NTU).

    Bicycle Riding Around the Taiwan Island in 11 days

    I challenged myself by organizing a bike ride with a few NTU students to circulate the Taiwan Island in 11 days (about 1,200 km). I was leading the students by riding in the front of the team to control the speed, take heed of the routes, and also helped fix broken gears.

    Director of Team Mentors

    In 2012, I served as Director of Team Mentors by leading 20 mentors to train 100 participated senior high school students in the CSIE Camp. The CSIE Camp is an annual event launched by undergraduate students from NTU CSIE to train senior high school students with basic computer science knowledge so as to motivate them to study computer science. Specifically, we advised these participants to collaboratively write a game agent in Python to play games and compete among all teams.

    Director of Public Relation

    In 2009 when I was a senior high school student, I served as Director of Public Relation in the Sign Language Club at the Affiliated Senior High School of National Taiwan Normal University. Specifically, I was in charge of coordinating with Club representatives from other senior high schools. I also organized a joint performance with 31 senior high schools for more than 1,300 audience.

    Chinese Calligraphy

    I started to practice Chinese calligraphy for 7 years when I was 9 years old. I am able to write in Chinese calligraphy in many kinds of styles, including Small Seal Script, Clerical Script, Regular Script, Semi-cursive Script, and Cursive Script. I love the delicate balance in the character structure and the drastic contrast in the writing intensity in Chinese calligraphy.
